Biography

Way, way back in the 20th Century, 3 members of former Iron Maiden singer Bruce Dickinson's band decided to cut their losses and set out on their own. Before long, Chris Dale (Bassist) put pen to paper and began to wirte. The songs that resulted were silly to say the least. The recording soon began, and joining Chris, Alex Dickson (Guitarist), and Alex "Sponder" Elena (Drummer), came a wonderful mixture of people from the music world, including Robin Guy (Drummer - Rachel Stamp), Doogie White (Vocals - Midnight Blue, Rainbow), and the entire of Class 4b of Berenger Sauniere Primary School. Before long, (if you take "long" as three years upwards) the songs were recorded and released in the debut album "(Music From) The Mystery Rabbits" (1999, ORG Records). The release was met with delightment by most, and confusion by all.

It wasn't long before the band began to write a new set of songs, this time with a slightly different vibe, and even more musicians involved. Soon Chris and the band had roped in the aid of the likes of David Ryder Prangley (Vocals - Rachel Stamp), and Jem Davis (Keyboardererer - Midnight Blue), and were recording Penguins On The Moon (2000, ORG Records). Considering it was the first time anyone had ever concieved an operatic musical about astronautic penguins eating cheese and falling in love with electrical appliances, the whole thing was quite a success.

After 4 more years of frustrating delays, the band's infamous Kiss covers album, Sheep In Kiss Make Up (2004, Sack Trick) was finally realeased in October '04. Featuring a host of Kiss classics with a whole new vibe to them, the album was met with delight by all. Countless musicians joined in the fun this time, most noteably Bruce Dickinson (Vocals - Iron Maiden), Reuben Gotto (Guitar - Sokratphorbia, Twin Zero), and Ben Calvert (Drums - Vex Red).
So what's next for Sack Trick? Only time will tell...
