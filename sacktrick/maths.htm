Maths Teacher's Words of Wisdom

When I was still to young to drink,
My teacher taught me how to think,
You can lead a horse to water but you can't paint him pink.
There's ne'er been a man of whom I'm fonder,
Absence makes your arms grow longer,
A rolling stone won't gather moths,
He was seldom never ever wrong.

OUR MATHS TEACHER SPEAKS WORDS OF WISDOM!
He's always got a wise reply;
OUR MATHS TEACHER SPEAKS WORDS OF WISDOM!
'Though his brains are made from apple pie!

All other teachers said he was past it,
Never put both legs in one basket,
An early bird is prone to catch worms, so always stay in bed 'till last.
Never look a clothes horse in the mouth,
Handsaws and hawks look the same in the south,
Out of sight is often behind,
He was the only hero of my youth!
An apple a day keeps the tigers away,
When the sun shines it's quite a nice day,
Spare the rod and boil the child,
I'd hang on every word he'd say!

He's always got a wise reply;

'Though his brains are made from apple pie!

OUR MATHS TEACHER SPEAKS WORDS OF WISDOM!
'Though we never found out why;
OUR MATHS TEACHER SPEAKS WORDS OF WISDOM!
With a paisley purple kipper tie!
